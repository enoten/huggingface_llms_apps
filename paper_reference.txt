Papers:

1. Yann Lecun's seminal paper on Conv NNs:
https://github.com/tpn/pdfs/blob/master/Gradient-Based%20Learning%20Applied%20to%20Document%20Recognition%20(Lecun98).pdf

Introduces filter embedded into NNs, symbolic derivative, etc.

2. How Deep NNs were pretrained in early days, transfer learning and LLM fine-tuning
https://github.com/enoten/huggingface_llms_apps/blob/main/Pre-training%20and%20Fine-tuning.ipynb

3. Seminal paper by Poggio and Riesenhuber, which discribes the multi-layer (deep) neural networks,
which mimics human brain from Primary Visual Cortex (V1) up to Inferior Temporal Cortex (IT).
https://www.hms.harvard.edu/bss/neuro/bornlab/nb204/papers/riesenhuber-poggio-hierarchical-nn1999.pdf

4. Paper by Larochelle, Bengio et. al explaining pretaining deep NNs using RBMs.
This is how the training of deep NNs was done in early days.
https://www.jmlr.org/papers/volume10/larochelle09a/larochelle09a.pdf

5. Similar to previous paper by Hinton et al. explaining how to train deep believe networks, 
how to train RBMs using Gibbs sampling, etc.
https://www.cs.toronto.edu/~hinton/absps/fastnc.pdf

6. Attention is all you need - a seminal paper about transformer networks
https://arxiv.org/pdf/1706.03762

7. Seminal paper introduce Back-Propagation Algorithm by Rumelhart, Hinton and Williams.
https://gwern.net/doc/ai/nn/1986-rumelhart-2.pdf

8. Paper about AlexNet, the first CNN to outperform other methods on ImageNet Dataset
https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2012/file/c399862d3b9d6b76c8436e924a68c45b-Paper.pdf

9. Seminal paper to introduce Skip-connection in NNs
this helps to stabilize deep NNs and make them very-very deep
https://arxiv.org/pdf/1512.03385

10. Paper about Growing neural networks and Widrow-Hoff learning rule (precursor of Back-Propagation)
